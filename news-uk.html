This suggests that, prior to all other recognised animals, dogs were domesticated. At this time, our canine companions were common in the northern hemisphere and had already broken into five distinct forms. Traces of these ancient ancestral breeds exist today in the Americas, Asia , Africa and Oceania, amid the expansion of European dogs during the colonial period. The thesis fills up some of the gaps in our close animal companions' natural history.
